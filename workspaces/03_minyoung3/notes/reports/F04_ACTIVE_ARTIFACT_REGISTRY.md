Latest interpretation: broad image signal is present, but the hippocampus-to-ventricle ratio far-positive label can be discordant with raw image geometry after normative adjustment. The residual is partly learnable from image-model outputs, but the first single-model residual-aware adapters convert that signal into near-cutoff/uncertain gains by sacrificing primary far-positive recall. Primary-preserving and monotonic residual overlays prevent most harm but do not produce significant gain over primary. Mask-derived ROI-union localization and fixed coarse ROI-token pooling are also closed as primary novelty directions: both beat fixed 2.5D but lose primary 3D far-boundary behavior. The primary-frozen weighted three-zone head also fails external OASIS/NACC validation as a method candidate: it preserves far-AUC through the frozen answer score but over-abstains far-positive rows. A primary-preserving overlay partly fixes this on OASIS, but NACC does not reproduce the gain and still significantly loses far-positive recall. The new morphometry-distillation auxiliary is mechanistically important because it learns ROI percentiles from images and improves some hip/MTL or far-negative structure at low weight, but every joint answer+aux variant fails the promotion gate by losing primary far-positive or far-boundary behavior. Primary teacher far-positive preservation does not fix this transfer problem. The frozen-primary morphometry probe is the cleanest current positive diagnostic: primary 3D features already encode ROI morphology well on held-out AJU while preserving near-primary answer behavior. The decoupled predicted-morphometry rule VQA experiment confirms that these image-derived ROI estimates can answer above 2.5D, but it still loses primary far-positive recall and far-AUC. Validation-only affine calibration improves binary AUC/bacc and reduces the raw rule's far-positive loss, but still fails primary non-inferiority; isotonic calibration overfits validation and transfers worse; asymmetric intervals/guardrails cannot repair the remaining deficit. Positive-margin weighted ROI regression moves the operating point and can increase far-positive recall in score-only mode, but ranking/far-AUC remains significantly worse and simultaneous uncertain/far-positive behavior is not recovered. Direct positive-focus pairwise rank supervision on the frozen morphometry head raises point zone-bacc and confirms 2.5D superiority again, but it still fails the primary gate because far-positive recall and far-AUC remain significantly worse; affine calibration after rank training worsens three-zone behavior. The raw-visible label audit is the strongest current diagnostic: the same image scores align substantially better with raw anatomy labels than adjusted normative labels, and primary 3D still strongly beats fixed 2.5D in raw-visible binary/far ranking. A balanced raw-visible subset audit confirms the AUC ordering even after class balancing, including hard negatives, but also shows that raw-visible operating thresholds must be recalibrated because 0.5 is inherited from adjusted-QA training. The validation-locked binary calibration audit confirms that global non-AJU thresholds recover hard-negative AJU 3D bacc above 2.5D, while questionwise calibration remains underpowered. The raw-visible benchmark construction pass confirms this at benchmark level: no split leakage, clinically matched negative selection where possible, and primary 3D clearly beats fixed 2.5D on AJU under global validation-locked calibration. Dedicated raw-visible training is stronger: cross-cohort seed synthesis over AJU/OASIS/NACC shows 3D beats fixed 2.5D in every evaluated seed by AUC and validation-locked calibrated bacc, with minimum cohort seed delta AUC +0.159 and bacc +0.146. Recall is not symmetric: OASIS positive recall is lower for 3D while negative recall is much higher, and NACC has one slightly negative positive-recall seed. Question-level synthesis localizes the caveat to operating policy rather than global representation failure: OASIS hippocampal/ventricle/ratio questions show positive-recall losses despite positive AUC/bacc gains, NACC ventricle has low AUC gain, and NACC ratio has the only negative-recall loss flag. Validation-locked pure 3D question-wise thresholding partially improves the OASIS caveat and raises OASIS bacc versus 3D global, but it still does not match 2.5D positive recall. The new subject-level bootstrap/calibration audit strengthens the promotable part: 3D question-wise versus 2.5D global has positive macro AUC and bacc CI lower bounds across AJU/OASIS/NACC, and 3D Brier score is lower in every cohort. It also locks the caveat: positive-recall CI is non-positive, especially OASIS. Recall-constrained pure-3D thresholding confirms that even when recall is prioritized, 3D keeps much higher specificity/bacc than 2.5D; OASIS floor 0.90 leaves 3D bacc +0.289 over 2.5D and negative recall +0.689, while positive recall remains -0.111 lower. Boundary-distance audit confirms that the 2.5D recall advantage is mostly a low-specificity operating point: OASIS 2.5D catches 29 positive rows missed by 3D but creates 170 false positives where 3D is true negative. The extra positives are near raw cutoff, while many false positives are farther from the positive cutoff. The persistent OASIS miss set after recall floors is small and now visualized: 4 sessions / 4 subjects, mostly ventricle enlargement, with raw percentile/score tables and central-slice montages. Matched failure panels show that same-question 3D true positives and 3D misses can have very similar raw percentiles/boundary distances but divergent 3D scores. The new ROI-local occlusion audit reproduces checkpoint scores and shows that 3D true positives have stronger MTL-branch local sensitivity than persistent 3D misses, whereas 2.5D slab sensitivity also appears on false positives. Validation-only score+sensitivity probing improves OASIS AUC/bacc/specificity but does not rescue positive recall at the default bacc point. The seed-stability audit refines the recall-floor story: at floor 0.95, score+sensitivity is non-degrading versus 3D score across all OASIS seeds and has mean bacc/positive-recall/negative-recall deltas +0.033/+0.011/+0.056, but it is not strictly positive in every seed. It remains much more specific than 2.5D at the same floor. A 2.5D positive-rescue hybrid is mixed and should remain diagnostic. Therefore the bottleneck is not absence of image morphology signal; it is operating risk near raw-visible cutoffs plus weak ROI-local score sensitivity in a small persistent OASIS positive miss set. Do not promote the current residual adapter, post-hoc residual overlays, ROI-union crop family, fixed-bin ROI-token residual family, primary-frozen uncertainty-head family, joint morphometry-distillation auxiliary family, predicted-morphometry rule/calibration/positive-margin/rank VQA family, 2.5D hybrid rescue, or current post-hoc sensitivity probe as method novelty. The next credible path is a targeted train-time ROI-local sensitivity/consistency method evaluated under the same LOCO seed protocol.

Latest addendum after train-time local evidence dropout: the targeted train-time ROI-local sensitivity experiment has now been run across the same OASIS seed protocol. Plain dropout improves validation-locked calibrated bacc in every seed and keeps positive recall non-degrading versus baseline 3D, but lowers AUC in every seed and has one negative-recall regression seed. Teacher-preserved dropout improves bacc in every seed and rescues seed20260603 AUC, but still lowers AUC in seeds 20260608/20260609 and has one positive-recall and one negative-recall regression seed. Focused near-boundary question-filtered dropout is worse in seed20260603 and is closed. Raising teacher preservation to 0.10 on the failing seed20260608 restores positive recall but not AUC or bacc gain, so stronger teacher preservation alone is also closed. A validation-only non-inferiority gate cleanly rejects the harmful teacher-local-evidence seeds and accepts only seed20260603, yielding non-degrading held-out OASIS AUC/bacc/positive/negative recall versus baseline 3D. Treat this as the best current operating-policy lead, but not yet method novelty until replicated beyond OASIS or integrated into model-selection protocol.

Latest addendum after external replication and frozen local-readout probes: NACC rejects the teacher-local-evidence objective, including a baseline-matched rerun, so destructive local erasing should not be treated as the next novelty path. Non-destructive frozen MTL-feature local readout is the cleaner mechanism signal: AJU/OASIS/NACC all show high validation local-readout AUC and all beat fixed 2.5D on held-out AUC/bacc/specificity, but the readout does not consistently beat the full baseline 3D path. Cross-cohort score-meta then shows that simple reliability meta-learning is also not method-ready: bacc/specificity improve versus baseline 3D in all cohorts, but AUC and positive recall are not non-degrading across cohorts. Row-level complementarity keeps the local-readout path alive: it rescues fewer baseline errors than 2.5D, but it damages far fewer baseline-correct rows, giving net +3 rows versus baseline while 2.5D gives net -55. The first validation-locked local override gate is negative: AJU ventricle gains do not transfer safely to NACC ventricle, and global override falls back to baseline in all cohorts. A conservative train-time teacher-preserved local-readout auxiliary is the best current mechanism lead because it improves held-out NACC AUC/bacc/specificity over baseline 3D, but it still loses positive recall and recall-constrained thresholding cannot fully repair that. The current scientific position is therefore: 3D raw-visible ROI-VQA has replicated image signal over 2.5D and ROI-local features are available, but a promotable method still needs a non-destructive score-generation objective that preserves positive recall under LOCO while keeping the observed specificity/ranking gain.

Latest addendum after far-positive and directional teacher preservation: raising teacher preservation specifically on far-positive rows produced a positive seed20260608 pilot, but the NACC three-seed replication demotes it from method lead to mechanism lead. One-sided directional teacher preservation repairs seed20260603 and makes strict AUC/bacc/negative-recall deltas versus baseline 3D nonnegative in all seeds, but positive recall remains nonnegative in only `1/3` seeds with minimum `-0.0625`. A focused positive-only directional pilot also fails seed20260608, with positive recall `-0.083` versus baseline 3D despite remaining above 2.5D by AUC/bacc. Fixed 2.5D remains the lower bar; the SCI/top-conference novelty gate is the stronger baseline 3D comparator with non-degrading positive recall.

## Next Step

The visual/QC morphology review and uncertainty-aware experiments show that 3D clearly beats the fixed 2.5D context, but most novelty candidates do not beat the tau=0.03 primary under AJU LOCO. Hard gain/loss inspection localizes the remaining problem to AJU MCI/CDR0.5 hippocampal and MTL cutoff-near cases. Generic SSL, local signed-distance auxiliaries, fixed ROI-token adapters, teacher preservation, answer masking, simple uncertainty auxiliary heads, direct three-zone CE, tri-view frozen direct heads, and validation-locked simple combos do not produce a promotable answer model. The primary-frozen weighted three-zone head remains the strongest new uncertainty-branch control because it preserves primary far-boundary AUC and significantly improves hip/MTL zone bacc over primary, but it still does not significantly beat original tri-view. Primary-frozen boundary-head and 2D decision-surface controls confirm the same scientific pattern: separating answer polarity from boundary uncertainty can raise some zone-bacc operating points and reduce far-positive over-abstention, but none significantly beats original tri-view or primary-frozen weighted head. The robust claims are therefore: 3D ROI-aware VQA should be framed as a three-zone anatomical reasoning task; 3D is substantially better than 2.5D for far-boundary answering and near-cutoff uncertainty identification; and global+local 3D fusion is better than either single 3D branch. External OASIS/NACC LOCO three-zone audit strengthens the 3D-vs-2.5D claim, with positive subject-level bootstrap CIs for primary and tri-view versus 2.5D across both cohorts. External error-structure audit shows the gain mechanism clearly: fixed 2.5D has zero uncertain recall, 3D recovers many near-cutoff uncertain rows, and remaining both-wrong rows concentrate close to the boundary. Far-positive guardrail and asymmetric-interval audits show this is not solved by another validation threshold or score interval: protecting far-positive recall still collapses much of the uncertain-row recovery. The next priority is to write the claim as a task/evaluation plus 3D representation result, not to overclaim the current uncertainty head as final method novelty. Any next model-side experiment must improve boundary representation quality, not only tune the decision surface, while preserving far-boundary AUC and far-positive recall. Model inputs must remain image tensors plus question ID only. Clinical fields, ROI values, evidence percentiles, scanner metadata, and AEB features are allowed only for label construction/audit/provenance, not model input. Outputs must remain under `results/f04_roi_evidence_encoder/`.
